A deal moves to Won in Pipedrive. The sales rep updates the stage, logs the call, makes a note. Their job is done.

Somewhere else in the business, operations needs to start preparing. Equipment to confirm, scheduling to begin, a customer to contact about logistics.

But nobody told them the deal closed.

## The informal handoff

Most businesses rely on some form of manual notification at this point. An email from the sales rep. A Slack message. A call. A shared spreadsheet that someone is supposed to check.

It works in the sense that operations usually finds out eventually. It doesn't work well.

The notification depends on the sales rep remembering to send it. It gets missed when people are busy. It arrives without the context operations actually needs. It has to be sent again when details change.

## When the handoff fails

The failure mode isn't usually a complete breakdown. It's a delay.

Operations finds out about the deal a day later than they should have. Setup starts late. [insert example — e.g. equipment allocation confirmed after customer had already been expecting confirmation]. The customer's first experience of the operations side of the business is a delay they can't explain.

The moment a deal is won is when the customer's expectations are highest. A failure at that handoff lands at exactly the wrong time.

## What the integration does

When a deal moves to Won in Pipedrive, an integration creates the corresponding opportunity in the operations system immediately. The relevant fields — customer details, dates, requirements — are mapped across.

Operations sees it before the sales rep has finished the call. No email to send, no Slack message to remember, no context to translate.

The sale and the delivery are two phases of the same job. The information should move between them automatically.
