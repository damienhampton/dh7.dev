Something goes wrong on a delivery. The address is wrong, or the customer isn't there, or the vehicle breaks down.

The driver knows immediately. The customer finds out shortly after — they were expecting a delivery that hasn't arrived.

Your office finds out when the driver calls in, or when the customer phones to complain.

That sequence is backwards.

## The data exists

Maxoptra and Samsara maintain real-time information on job status, vehicle location, route progress, and exception events. When a delivery is marked failed, when a vehicle deviates from its route, when a job is running significantly late — the system knows.

The problem isn't that the information doesn't exist. It's that it's sitting in a platform that not everyone in the business is actively monitoring.

A dispatcher watching the Maxoptra screen can see problems as they happen. But the office manager, the account manager, the customer service team — they find out through a different channel, usually a reactive one.

## Proactive rather than reactive

An integration that pushes status updates out of the routing and telemetry systems closes this gap.

A job completed early surfaces immediately in an ops dashboard. A failed delivery triggers an alert before the driver has even moved on to the next stop. A vehicle running significantly behind schedule is visible before the customer calls to ask where they are.

Your customers shouldn't be the ones telling you something went wrong.
