During a working day, a dispatcher wants to know where things stand. Which jobs are done. Which are running late. Whether anything has gone wrong.

The traditional answer is to call the driver. Or wait for them to call in.

This works in the sense that information eventually arrives. It is reactive, time-consuming, and depends on the driver having a moment to stop and communicate clearly whilst in the middle of a job.

## The information is already there

Maxoptra and Samsara maintain real-time state on jobs, routes, vehicle locations, and completion events. When a job is marked delivered, the timestamp is recorded. When a vehicle deviates from its planned route, the system knows. When a job is flagged as failed or incomplete, that status exists in the platform.

The information is there. The question is whether it's reaching the people who need it, or sitting in a system that not everyone is checking.

A dispatcher watching the screen has it. The office manager, the account manager, the customer service team — they typically don't, until someone tells them.

## Pushing information to where people are

An integration that reads events from Maxoptra or Samsara and routes them to a shared destination changes the dynamic.

Completed jobs surface in an ops dashboard immediately, without the driver calling in. A failed delivery triggers an alert — to a Slack channel, to a shared inbox, to whoever needs to act — before the customer has had a chance to call and complain. A vehicle running significantly behind schedule is visible to the whole team before the first customer enquiry arrives.
