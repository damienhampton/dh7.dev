# Integration as a business and the impact of AI

There is so much great software out that small businesses rely on, covering everything from project management, customer relationship management, sales, logistics, stock control, warehousing, finance - you name, there will be something for it.

In most cases, a business will likely rely on more than one piece of software. If they are lucky, the different software will integrate together, making everything easy. If they do not integrate, maybe there will be some glue like Zapier or Pipedream that will connect them.

There are times when a business relies of two pieces of software that do not integrate - and this is where problems start. When key pieces of software cannot talk to each other, manual processes are used. Manual processes are slow and error prone and a cost sink.

Even if the software integrates, there might still be processes that need to be run manually, that could be automated to reduce friction and free up team resources.

This is the world of custom integrations. The problem with custom integrations is that they are expensive and they can be brittle. They are expensive because you do not get the economy of scale of building for hundreds of users and sharing the costs. You are building for one user and you bear all the costs. They can be brittle because, if you are integrating two systems, then it only takes one provider to change their API for the integration to break. Fixing the integration then can take time and cost more money.

Historically, this was only really feasible for larger companies, where they could absorb the costs.

I believe that AI has now changed the game. AI allows us to get more done with less, which lowers the barrier to entry for small businesses.

It has never been easier or more practical to integrate business systems - as long as they have useable APIs.

I would still recommend using off-the-shelf integrations where possible, but if you can simplify your business with a custom integration, dashboard or utility then it is worth considering.
